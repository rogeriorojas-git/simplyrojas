The ancient surname of Rojas...

The ancient surname of Roxas, Rojas or de Rojas originated in the city of Rojas, Burgos. It spread from there to Palencia, Toledo and Andalucia. One of the first to take this name was Diego Sanchez, who owned the village of Rojas, in Bribiesca, Burgos. He lived in the late 1100s.

There were several Rojas families who presented themselves before the various Offices of the Holy Inquisition. Agustin de Rojas and his wife, Juana del Valle, submitted their genealogy. No date or place is given. Fernando Rojas y Gonzalez Cobo, of Curiel, petitioned in 1673 for the position of Official de Valladolid. Baptismal certificates and wills are contained in this petition. Fray Francisco Rojas, of the Order of San Francisco and a native of Toledo, in 1623 petitioned for the position of Calificador del Consejo in Toledo; Gaspar Rojas Victoria, a native of Guadalajara, and his wife Francisca Rivera, of Granada, in 1593 petitioned for the position of Familiar in Mexico City. Jose de Rojas ORtega, a native of Huamanga, Peru, petitioned the Inquisition in Lima in 1730 for the office of Familiar. Juan de Rojas, petitioned the Inquisition at Cartagena de Indias in 1645 for the office of Comisario. In 1621, Pedro de Rotas Valdes, native of Tinco, resident of Manila, and his wife, Juana de la Cueva Herrera, native of Manila, petitioned in Mexico City for the office of Familiar.

Family histories have been identified for the Rojas families in Argentina, Chile, Colombia, Cuba, the Dominican Republic and Venezuela.

In the United States, Rojas is the 73rd most popular surname among modern Hispanic homilies. In Santa Cruz, Arizona, in 1821, Gavian Rojas lived with her husband, Ramon Montiel and their two children, Toribio and Josefa. Felipe Rojas was the husband of Barbara Figueroa. Living in the same household was Juliana Rojas, who might have been Felipe's sister. Alvina Rojas was the wife of Jose Romero.

In San Antonio, Texas, Juan Jose de Rojas served as a witness in 1722. He had more than 15 years of military service. He had served under the Marques de Aguayo before the expedition into Texas, and he had accompanied the Marques in the founding of the royal presidio of Adaes. He was in San Antonio again in 1726. He received a grant in 1741. His wife was Nicolasa Trevino. Their children were Juana Gertrudis, Basilio and Rosalia. Another San Antonio family was that of Joseph Antonio de Rojas, who was there in 1731. He married Maria Curbelo and they had four children. One of them, Franciso, married Antonia Granado, and the couple had nine children. These Rojas families have many descendants throughout Texas. In Nacogdoches in 1805, Nicolas Rojas, a Spanish farmer, age 38, was marriedto Dorotea, a French woman, age 49(?). They had two sons and two daughters, a grandson, and three granddaughters.

In 1784 in Pensacola, Florida, Josef de Roxas and his wife, Antonia Felipe, were the parents of Josef, Melchor, Francisco, Pedro, and Maria. There was also a Maria de Roxas, who was the wife of Miguel Quintana.

Don Franciso de Rojas y Guevara, was appointed Conde de Mora in 1602. Don Jeronimo de Rojas y Rojas, became Marques de la Pena de los Enamorados in 1679. Don Pedro de Rojas y Contreras was appointed Marques de Villanueva de Duero in 1740. Don Jose de Rojas y Contreras became Marques de Albentos in 1761, and Don Eduardo de Rojas y Alonso was appointed Conde de Montarco in 1879.
